About this lecture series

Category Theory is an exciting field of mathematics that studies analogies and abstractions. Through it it's possible to formalize the meaning of similarity and to better understand the many different kinds of similarities, besides allowing one to generalize powerful theorems from many (surprisingly) different domains.

Lately through the field of Applied Category Theory (ACT) it's being applied beyond Maths, to domains like Computer Science, Physics, Natural Language Processing, Control Theory and, most importantly for us, Machine Learning.

Yet despite Category Theory's immense flexibility and power it's still mostly unknown by Machine Learning Researchers. This lecture series aims to help spread the knowledge and help increase the use of Categories in Machine Learning, and so lead to a different way to frame and understand the amazing power and challenges in the field.

Who is this event for?

This event is mainly focused on AI researchers and practicioners, but anyone interested in the fields of Machine Learning and Category Theory is welcome to watch the videos ask questions and join our discord community!

We hope to provide a rich discussion environment in which the participants can discover and learn more about the interface between the two fields. Besides, this event is an opportunity to improve and deepen their understanding of ML and CT and positively impact future research.
